Package 1
Tax Reform
Capital Gains Tax  |  Proposed from 1 July 2027
CGT Reform: Indexation Replaces the 50% Discount

Currently, assets held 12+ months get a 50% CGT discount. From 1 July 2027, this is proposed to be replaced with CPI-based cost base indexation, meaning you only pay tax on the gain above inflation. A 30% minimum tax rate on net capital gains is also proposed.

Proposed to cover all asset classes: shares, property, business assets and pre-1985 assets held by individuals, trusts and partnerships. Only gains accruing from 1 July 2027 are affected.

  • New residential builds: choice of 50% discount or new indexation method on sale
  • Main residence: CGT exemption fully retained
  • Four small business CGT concessions: retained and unchanged
  • Qualifying affordable housing: existing 60% CGT discount retained
  • Income support recipients (Age Pension, JobSeeker): proposed to be exempt from the 30% minimum tax
  • Super funds including SMSFs: retain existing one-third discount, not affected
Key Notes on Cost Base
  • All pre-existing assets may require a market revaluation as at 1 July 2027, subject to draft legislation and subsequent ATO guidance, either by independent valuation or an ATO apportionment formula.
  • Renovations and capital improvements: each capital improvement is its own cost base element, indexed from its own expenditure date. Keep all invoices and receipts.
Example: Property with Renovation

Sam buys an investment property on 1 July 2027 for $500,000. In 2030, Sam spends $100,000 on a renovation. Sam sells on 1 July 2035 for $900,000. Assumed CPI factor: purchase to sale = 1.20; renovation to sale = 1.15.

Purchase price$500,000
Indexed purchase cost (x 1.20)$600,000
Renovation cost$100,000
Indexed renovation cost (x 1.15)$115,000
Total indexed cost base$715,000
Sale proceeds$900,000
Taxable gain (real gain only)$185,000

At a 47% marginal rate, tax is approx. $86,950. The 30% minimum floor ($55,500) is already exceeded. Without indexation (old 50% discount), taxable gain would have been $150,000.

Negative Gearing  |  Proposed from 1 July 2027
Negative Gearing: Restricted to New Builds

From 1 July 2027, negative gearing against salary or other non-property income would generally be limited to new builds only.

Losses from affected established residential properties could only offset residential property income, including future capital gains, with unused losses carried forward.

Transitional Arrangements (Announced 12 May 2026)
  • Held at or under contract at 7:30pm AEST 12 May 2026: fully grandfathered, current rules apply until sold
  • Contracted after 7:30pm AEST 12 May 2026 and before 1 July 2027: may negatively gear during that period only
  • Contracted from 1 July 2027: losses cannot offset other taxable income
  • New builds (all dates): full negative gearing retained
  • Commercial property and shares: not affected
  • Widely held trusts and super funds (incl. SMSFs): excluded
Workers  |  Proposed from 2027-2028
$250 Working Australians Tax Offset (WATO)

Over 13.3 million Australian workers are proposed to receive a new $250 annual tax offset from the 2027-2028 income year, reducing tax payable.

Combined with previously legislated tax cuts, workers on average earnings may see up to $2,816 per year in total tax reduction across all five measures.

Workers  |  Proposed from 2026-2027
$1,000 Instant Work-Related Expense Deduction

Workers are proposed to be able to claim a standard $1,000 deduction for work-related expenses without itemising or retaining receipts.

Expected to benefit 6.2 million workers with an average tax saving of approximately $205.

Discretionary Trusts  |  Proposed from 1 July 2028
30% Minimum Tax on Discretionary Trust Distributions

From 1 July 2028, the trustee is proposed to pay a minimum 30% tax on the trust's total taxable income. Beneficiaries (other than corporate beneficiaries) would receive non-refundable credits for the tax the trustee has paid.

Proposed exemptions (with some exceptions, including but not limited to)

  • Primary production (farming) income
  • Fixed and widely held trusts
  • Super funds, special disability trusts, deceased estates, charitable trusts
  • Testamentary trusts (income from assets existing at announcement date)

Bucket companies

Corporate beneficiaries (bucket companies) would not receive credits for trustee-level tax, removing the ability to convert those into refundable franking credits.

Franking credits held by the trust

Where a trust holds shares and receives franked dividends, the trustee is expected to use those franking credits to pay the minimum tax first. What happens when franking credits exceed the minimum tax liability is still subject to consultation and has not yet been finalised.

Example: Family Trust Distribution

A family trust earns $200,000. The trustee splits it: $50,000 to each of 4 family members. Today, each pays tax at their own rate, say 19%, total family tax approx. $38,000.

Under the proposed rules, the trustee pays 30% on $200,000 = $60,000, regardless of how the income is split. Beneficiaries get a non-refundable credit for their share.

If a beneficiary's personal tax rate is already 30% or above, their overall tax position is broadly unchanged.

Rollover relief

3 years from 1 July 2027 to restructure out of a discretionary trust (e.g. into a company or fixed trust) without triggering CGT or income tax.

Package 2
Productivity and Business
Companies  |  Proposed from 1 July 2026
Loss Carry Back: Reintroduced Permanently

A company with a tax loss this year is proposed to be able to claim a cash refund against corporate tax paid in the previous two income years.

  • Companies with global turnover under $1 billion
  • Revenue losses only (not capital losses)
  • Expected to benefit up to 85,000 companies
Example

A business paid $12,500 tax on a $50,000 profit in 2025-2026. In 2026-2027 it reports a $15,000 loss. It carries back the loss and receives a $3,750 refund ($15,000 x 25%) from prior year tax paid.

Start-Ups  |  Proposed from 1 July 2028
Loss Refundability for New Small Businesses

New small businesses in their first 2 years are proposed to be able to convert tax losses into immediate cash refunds.

  • Businesses with aggregated turnover up to $10 million
  • Refund capped at: PAYG withholding + FBT paid to Australian employees that year
  • Expected to benefit approximately 25,000 companies per year
Example: PAYG Cap

A start-up remits $40,000 PAYG and $5,000 FBT. It reports a $200,000 tax loss. The refund is capped at $45,000 (PAYG + FBT). The remaining $155,000 loss carries forward as normal.

Small Business  |  Permanent from 1 July 2026
$20,000 Instant Asset Write-Off: Made Permanent

Small businesses are proposed to immediately deduct any eligible asset under $20,000 in the year of purchase rather than depreciating over time.

  • Businesses with aggregated turnover up to $10 million
  • Per asset limit, each asset assessed individually
  • Expected $890 million cash flow improvement over 5 years
R&D  |  Proposed from 1 July 2028
R&D Tax Incentive: Overhaul
  • Core R&D offset proposed to increase to approximately 50% (from ~25%)
  • Refundable offset threshold raised to $50 million turnover (for firms under 10 years old)
  • Annual expenditure cap raised to $200 million
  • Intensity threshold reduced to 1.5%, broadening eligibility
  • Minimum expenditure of $50,000 required
Electric Vehicles  |  Phased from 1 April 2027
EV FBT: Three-Stage Transition
  • EVs under $75,000: full FBT exemption continues, provided the arrangement commences before 1 April 2029
  • From 1 April 2027: EVs over $75,000 move to a 25% FBT discount
  • From 1 April 2029: all eligible EVs move to the 25% FBT discount (full exemption ends)
Example

Employer provides an EV under $75,000 via novated lease starting before 1 April 2029. The arrangement retains the full FBT exemption until 1 April 2029, then a 25% discount on the taxable value is expected to apply.

Investment and Regulatory Reform
Venture Capital and Red Tape Reduction
  • VC incentives (ESVCLP and VCLP) proposed to expand from 1 July 2027
  • 497 tariffs abolished from 1 July 2026, saving businesses est. $157 million per year
  • Financial sector compliance costs expected to reduce by $780 million per year via 14 legislative reforms
  • Optional monthly PAYG instalments from 1 July 2027

Package 3
Savings and Stability
NDIS Reform
$37.8 billion in savings over the next 4 years. The Government describes these as necessary to keep the scheme sustainable long-term.
Fuel and Energy Security
$14.8 billion plan including a permanent Australian Fuel Security Reserve. From 1 July 2027, LNG exporters expected to reserve 20% of export volumes for the domestic market.
Foreign Investment
Foreign Residential Property Ban Extended to 2029

The ban on foreign investors purchasing established residential properties is proposed to be extended to 2029. New builds remain open to foreign investment.

Skills and Housing
Workforce and Housing Supply Measures
  • $85.2 million to fast-track skills assessments for migrant trades workers
  • Last-mile infrastructure funding to unlock up to 65,000 new homes
  • Free access to building standards referenced in Australian legislation
  • Free TAFE training to support construction workforce supply
